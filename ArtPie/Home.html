Who are we?

Only the best record players will do ​for true music lovers. While the sounds are fantastic, record players lack the art illusion and dynamic visuals which are keen to the eye. 

This is why we reimagined what a record player can be. Our project is an interactive integrated mechanical and electrical system that uses computer vision to detect objects by shape and transform such into audiovisual scenes. 

Dynamic Art

Art tells stories which can be interpreted in many forms. This player tells stories through moving contours!

Audiovisual

The player generates personalized audio and light shows inside the enclosure based on object geometry.

Our clean product finish appeals to a wider audience.

Our Process

For this project, we completed a sequence of three two week-long sprints to design a mechatronic system. For each sprint, we completed an integrated system that progressively built out the final product. At the end of the each two week-long sprint, we held sprint reviews.

Do you wish to recreate our project?

You are in luck, check out our materials list below! 

Note that while this project is intended for use on a Raspberry Pi integrated with the rest of our mechanical and electrical systems, it is possible to run it locally with limited functionality.

For best results, we suggest having the following:
  1. A webcam
  2. A red laser line
  3. A means of playing audio
  4. A Fadecandy board with some WS2812b LEDs

Meet Our Team

Zoie Le​o

Zoie is passionate about the overlap of visual design and engineering. Throughout this project she learned about the wonderous world of Adobe Illustrator and how to force the program to collaborate with temperamental SolidWorks files. She is also interested in user interaction with form, specifically in the form of record players. Before this project she was not a frequent user of record players, but now, it is a whole different story.

Jasper Katzban

J​asper enjoys mixing sound and software together to design things that make us appreciate the physical environment in new ways. In this project, he was excited for the opportunity to learn about computer vision stacks, software systems, and integration across electrical, software, and mechanical domains. He thinks it was especially fun to write software that gives everyday objects their own sonic signature!

Priscila Morales
